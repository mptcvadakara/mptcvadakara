Training & Placement Cell

About Training & Placement Cell

A Training and Placement Cell (T&P Cell) is a dedicated department within educational institutions that acts as a bridge between students and industry, focusing on enhancing employability and securing job opportunities. It conducts training, workshops, and mock interviews while organizing on-campus recruitment drives to connect students with employers. This cell ensures that students are prepared for the competitive job market, bridging the gap between academic learning and corporate expectations.

Objectives

  1. Training & Skill Development: Enhances students' industry readiness through training in soft skills (communication, leadership), aptitude tests (quantitative, logical), and technical proficiency.
  2. Placement & Recruitment: Manages the entire recruitment lifecycle, including sending invitations to companies, managing logistics (interview rooms, online tests), and ensuring student placement.
  3. Career Guidance & Counseling: Helps students identify their strengths and weaknesses, align their interests with career options, and set professional goals.
  4. Industry-Institute Interaction: Establishes and maintains relationships with companies for industrial visits, guest lectures, and internships to keep students updated on industry trends.
  5. Documentation & Data Maintenance: Maintains comprehensive databases of student profiles, resumes, and company information to facilitate the hiring process.
  6. Structured Organization: Often headed by a Placement Officer and supported by staff and student representatives from various departments.
  7. Conduct:To take care of the discipline and course and conduct in the campus.
